One of the things I have been really enjoying during my [career break] is learning to tackle [cryptic crosswords][national-crossword-solvers-day]. I am still not very good at them, but I can solve clues today that were dark to me a year ago, and that's pleasing progress.

I was delighted to see that the authors of the [*Minute Cryptic*][Minute Cryptic] book I mentioned on [National Crossword Solvers Day][national-crossword-solvers-day] are about to release a second book and were hosting a [workshop] at [Melbourne Writers Festival] at the weekend. I pedalled down to the city after [timekeeping at Parkville parkrun][timekeeping-at-parkville] on Saturday and had a lovely time hearing about how Minute Cryptic got started and how it's growing.

![Photo of Liam and Angas presenting at MWF][image]

One thing I have come to appreciate about cryptic clues is that there's a very fine line between some clues and ~~Dad~~ _bad_ jokes. Perhaps that's where some of the appeal comes from. There were a lot of audible groans in the room during the workshop as solutions were revealed to clues like:

> One goes into debt flying private jet? (5)

You can see [the solution to this] in the [Minute Cryptic YouTube channel].

(Yes, _DJ_, I met a "YouTuber"!)

After this experience, I was inspired to have a go at writing a couple of clues this week, which I am sharing here for you.

> ["Fe-female!", made heavy metal fans run to the hills! (4, 6)][Fe-female!]

> [Complete lack of lighting at the weekend made concertgoers paranoid (5, 7)][paranoid]

[Let me know how you go!]
